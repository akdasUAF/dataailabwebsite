Our projects that impact Alaska

AI to Mitigate Environmental Impact on Human Health

We develop technologies to analyze environmental hazard data such as harmful algal blooms, forest fire, etc. helping to protect public health in Alaska. By using machine learning models, authorities can identify high-risk areas to take proactive measures to prevent exposure to toxic algae.

AI for Forest Fire

We used power of AI to integrate satellite data, and microbial information to predict forest fire risk by analyzing environmental conditions and microbial activity linked to fire outbreaks. This approach also helps assess forest dynamics by monitoring changes in vegetation health and biodiversity, providing early warning systems for forest management.

Big data Metagenomics

We are developing an HPC-compatible big data pipeline that enables efficient extraction of pangenomic information of a particular microbial community from large-scale metagenomic datasets that contain diverse microbiome samples. This pipeline leverages high performance computing, and distributed and parallel processing to analyze complex microbial communities, facilitating insights into gene diversity, functional potential, and microbial interactions

Big Data Water Informatics

We are developing a cutting-edge data engineering pipeline utilizing the power of HPC, container technologies, NoSQL databasesand AI-power webscraping technologies to collect scattered water and waste water information and running analytics on it to optimize water quality monitoring and management systems, ensuring reliable sanitation services across Alaska. By integrating data, predictive analytics, and AI-driven insights, this pipeline enhances decision-making and promotes sustainable water resource management.

Accesibility of Data/AI/HPC Education

We are working towards improved accessibility to Data/AI/HPC education in remote Alaska. We develop novel online learning platforms and host remote workshops in partnerships with local organizations. This will help bridge the digital divide, offering residents the skills and resources to engage with cutting-edge technologies for research and economic growth.
